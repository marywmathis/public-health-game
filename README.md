# You're in Charge: Public Health Decisions

An interactive classroom activity for high school students. Students play the role of a public health decision-maker through 10 realistic scenarios across health literacy, outbreaks, equity, and mixed applied topics (mental health, climate, vaccines, nutrition).

## How to use

- **In class:** Open the live site, project it on the board. Read each case aloud, let students vote (hands, sticky notes, or phones in chat), then click the answer they pick. The reveal shows what evidence suggests and why other answers were tempting.
- **Self-paced:** Share the URL with students directly.
- **Offline:** Download `index.html` and open it in any browser. No internet required after that.

## Run time

About 25-30 minutes for all 10 cases, with discussion. Stop anywhere — each case is self-contained.

## Topics covered

- Round 1: Health literacy (evaluating health claims, understanding studies)
- Round 2: Outbreak investigation and public communication
- Round 3: Health equity and social determinants
- Round 4: Applied scenarios — mental health, climate & health, vaccine confidence, food access

## Teaching notes

The cases are designed so that "wrong" answers are still defensible — they're things real officials actually try. The point isn't memorization; it's helping students see the tradeoffs public health professionals weigh every day.

A few cases (especially the food desert and climate ones) have answers experts genuinely disagree on. Lean into that. The exit ticket explicitly invites students to argue against the "correct" answer.
